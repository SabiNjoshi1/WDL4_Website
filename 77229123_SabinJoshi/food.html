Drinking Water

It is logical to think so, with so many rivers and himalayan springs. However, unfortunately, this is not true. There are rivers and springs of course, but fresh water is untreated. You may be able to drink it for fun but not at health risk. It is therefore often prudent to have purified bottles of mineral water available everywhere.

About Food

During trekking in Nepal the food is mostly about Daal-Bhaat and the appetite is nothing better. Usually, the rice-and-lentil-soup is eaten with one or two kinds of vegetables, pickles, and an optional chicken or mutton made to order. In every tea-house on the trek it's on the menu. Many lodges and hotels sell small Western dishes including muesli, instant soup, breads, pizza, curry, scrambled eggs, toast, spaghetti and even ice-cream and cakes.
